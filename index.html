The problem

Three urban crises, one solution

Asian cities face mounting pressure from dusty roads, extreme surface heat, and untreated wastewater. We turn the problem into its own answer.

🌫

Dusty roads

Unpaved roads generate fine particulate matter that harms respiratory health, especially in densely populated urban areas.

🌡

Urban heat island

Asphalt raises local temperatures 3–8 °C. Wet surfaces cool naturally through evaporation — for free.

💧

Wasted wastewater

Urban institutions discharge grey water that flows untreated into drains every day — a free resource going completely to waste.

How it works

Collect → Filter → Sprinkle → Cool

An ESP32-controlled pipeline monitors water quality and road temperature, activating only when all safety conditions are met.

01

Collection

Intake pump draws wastewater from grey-water outlets into a holding tank.

02

Filtration

Three-stage filter removes particles, chemicals, and odour from the water.

03

Sensing

Turbidity, temperature, and level sensors verify all conditions before activating.

04

Sprinkling

Solenoid valve opens and mist nozzles spray the road surface.

Stage 01

Coarse mesh screen

Removes solids and debris. Protects pumps from clogging.

Stage 02

Activated carbon

Absorbs dissolved chemicals, chlorine, and odour compounds.

Stage 03

Sand & gravel bed

Final polish. Removes fine suspended particles.

Output check

Turbidity sensor

Confirms water clarity at output. ESP32 blocks valve if below threshold.
